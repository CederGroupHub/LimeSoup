    The reason for this change is that water transported by electro-osmotic drag across the membrane to the cathode was mistakenly accounted for twice in the original manuscript. Water carried by the protons was assumed to be injected into the pores of the cathode catalyst layer at the catalyst layer and membrane inter face (see the fourth equation in the fourth column in Table II and Eq. 16). However, this amount of water was taken into account again by the term <mml:math display="inline"><mml:mrow><mml:mn>4</mml:mn><mml:msubsup><mml:mi>n</mml:mi><mml:mi mathvariant="normal">d</mml:mi><mml:mi>CL</mml:mi></mml:msubsup><mml:msub><mml:mi>R</mml:mi><mml:mrow><mml:msub><mml:mi mathvariant="normal">O</mml:mi><mml:mrow><mml:mn>2</mml:mn></mml:mrow></mml:msub></mml:mrow></mml:msub></mml:mrow></mml:math>4ndCLRO2 (water carried by the protons is released as the protons are consumed in oxygen reduction reaction in the catalyst layer) in the original governing equation within the catalyst layer.

    The program was rerun using the same set of parameter values as in the original manuscript, and the correct simulation results are provided below. For comparison, the original curve for the base case is also included in the corrected Fig. 1 (dashed line). One can see that a higher mass-transfer-limiting current is obtained as a result from this change because less water is generated in the catalyst layer of the cathode. This change also affects the performance and local profiles in the case studies. However, it does not change the trends of the effects of the parameters of interest, and consequently, the conclusions drawn from the previous results are still valid. (See Fig. 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 and 8.)
