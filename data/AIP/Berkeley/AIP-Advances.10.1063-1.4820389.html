Abstract

The structural and electronic properties of fully hydrogenated SiC graphane-like nano-structures have been investigated. The objective of this study is to underscore the relative stability of different conformers of hydrogenated SiC sheet. All calculations are carried out using plane wave based pseudo-potential approach under the density functional theory. The results reveal that the fully hydrogenated SiC sheet forms five stable isomers, and the chair conformer is most stable. Further study through molecular dynamic simulation strategy demonstrates that even at room temperature the chair conformer remains stable.

B 46, 6671 (1992). https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevB.46.6671 For the calculation of the hydrogenated SiC graphane-like sheet, a periodic super cell of length 12.32 Å × 10.68 Å is used as a model. The sheet is constructed with 16 Si and 16 C atoms placed alternatively and H atoms are attached with each Si and C atoms. While the sheet is periodically repeated in the X- and Y-direction, a large vacuum of 15 Å is left between two layers in Z-direction. For good convergence of the total energy the energy “cut-off” is set to 600 eV and the Brillouin zones (BZ) are sampled with 5 × 5 × 1 k-point grid. The k-mesh is generated by Monkhorst–Pack2323. H. J. Monkhorst and J. D. Pack, Phys. Rev. B 13, 5188 (1976). https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevB.13.5188 method and all the results are tested for convergence with respect to mesh size. In all our calculations, self-consistency is achieved with 0.001 eV convergences in total energy. The optimization of each structure is carried out by minimizing the forces less than 0.001 eV/Å.
The SiC sheet can be viewed as a 2D array of repetitive hexagonal SiC units in the same plane. In the SiC sheet each Si and C atom is bonded with three C and Si atoms, respectively. Thus the final structure of the SiC sheet adopts graphene-like honeycomb pattern. Y. Miyamoto et al.,2424. Y. Miyamoto and B. D. Yu, Appl. Phys. Lett. 80, 586 (2002). https://doi.org/10.1063/1.1445474 reported that SiC graphene-like structure with alternate Si-C bond (sp2 type), is energetically the most favorable.
To start with, we have optimized the clean SiC sheet without any symmetry constraint. This result is important to analyze further studies, where the clean SiC sheet has been hydrogenated. For the SiC sheet, we have used a periodic super-cell of 12.32 × 10.68 Å in the plane and 12 Å separation between two SiC planes. This large separation is required to avoid any image interation between two planes. After full ionic relaxation [Fig. 1(a)], the cell parameters of the hexagonal lattice is found to be a1 = a2 = 3.082 Å. We have noted that all the Si-C bonds in the SiC sheet are identical with bond distance of 1.78 Å which is in accordance with the earlier report.2424. Y. Miyamoto and B. D. Yu, Appl. Phys. Lett. 80, 586 (2002). https://doi.org/10.1063/1.1445474 In this context it should be mentioned that the Si-C bond length in the SiC sheet (1.78 A) is smaller than the bulk SiC (1.89 Å). The difference in the bond length is attributed to the difference in hybridization. While the hybridization of the Si and C atoms in the sheet is sp2, it is sp3 for the bulk.

The binding energy of the SiC sheet is calculated by the following equation.
BE Si -C/ atom =(E total −16E Si −16EC)/2n(1)
Where, Etotal is the total energy after optimization, n is the number of Si or C atom in the super cell, ESi and EC are the corrected spin polarized atom energy of Si and C atoms, respectively. By replacing the energy values in the above equation the stabilization energy of Si-C sheet/atom is estimated to be 5.815 eV. Now to analyze the charge density distribution in the planar sheet we have plotted the iso-density surface of the SiC sheet as shown in Fig. 1(b).
In order to calculate the points charges on each atom, a separate SCF calculation using the LCAO-MO based approach was carried out on the same SiC sheet after terminating the edges by H atoms. The point charge distribution has been found out employing the Mulliken population analysis scheme using GAMESS software.2525. M. W. Schmidt et al., J. Comput. Chem. 14, 1347 (1993). https://doi.org/10.1002/jcc.540141112 It is seen that considerable amount of electronic charge (0.415e) is transferred from Si to the nearby C atoms. To further corroborate the electronic charge distribution and also to underscore the details of the electronic structure, the total density of states (DOS) of the SiC sheet, and the orbital decomposed DOS (s-DOS and p-DOS) of the constituent Si and C atoms are plotted in Fig. 2. The energy gap between the occupied and unoccupied energy levels is found to be 2.42 eV. Moreover, the orbital decomposed DOS suggests that the Fermi energy level of the SiC sheet is constructed by the p-orbital electrons of carbon atoms, and the lowest energy levels of the unoccupied states is formed by the p orbital electrons of Si atoms.

After establishing the structural and electronic properties of the clean SiC sheet we now explore the stability and electronic structure of hydrogenated conformers of the SiC sheet. Depending upon the placement of hydrogen atoms, various conformations of the hydrogenated SiC sheet (with formula unit SiHCH) can be generated. However, all of them may not be energetically favorable. In this work we have considered eight different structural configurations, where the SiC sheet was fully hydrogenated. The hydrogen atoms are attached to both Si and C atoms in different orientations. All these geometries of hydrogenated SiC sheets are relaxed fully without any symmetry constraint. In order to make the reading comfortable, in the following section we briefly describe each configurations and their corresponding nomenclature.
In the initial geometries hydrogen atoms are placed on the hexagonal honeycomb structure of the SiC sheet as: (1) “One up- one down alternatively,” giving rise to the chair conformer after full ionic relaxation, (2) “Two up-one down followed by two up-one down,” leading to form the boat conformer in the optimized geometry; (3) “Three up-one down followed by one up-one down,” giving rise to the puckered conformer in the optimized geometry. (4) “Two up-two down followed by one-up-one-down,” giving rise to the twist conformer in the optimized geometry. (5) “Three adjacent hydrogen atoms are at the top side and next three hydrogen atoms are at the down side,” giving rise to form the stirrup conformer after ionic relaxation. (6) “all up or all down” forms an stable isomer with very high energy with respect to the most stable chair form (7) “four up-two down” leading to form unstable isomer; (8) “five up-one down” also leading form an unstable isomer. Thus, on the basis of the formation energy criteria, we could identify five energetically stable conformers of fully hydrogenated SiC sheet.
The initial and final structures of these isomers are shown in Fig. 3. The stable conformers are named as chair, boat, puckered, twist and stirrup conformers of SiHCH sheets. The optimized geometrical parameters (bond angles and bond lengths) of all the stable conformers are listed in Table I. It is seen that for the chair conformer, the <H-Si-C and <H-C-Si are 108.2°, conforming a regular sp3 hybridization of Si and C atoms. But in other conformers, both the bond angles are smaller than the regular sp3 angles. The inter-atomic distance between Si and C atoms in the chair conformer is 1.87 Å. In this context it may be recalled that the Si-C bond lengths for the clean SiC sheet and bulk SiC crystals are 1.78 Å and 1.89 Å, respectively. Therefore, the difference in the bond length of Si-C in the clean sheet (1.78 Å) and hydrogenated sheet with chair conformation (1.87 Å) is clearly a manifestation of the change in the nature of chemical bonding or in other words the change in the hybridization. For boat and stirrup conformers, two types of Si-C distances are found. For the boat conformer, the Si-C bonds connecting the H atoms, which are placed on the same side of the sheet, are larger (1.92 Å) than the bonds joining H atoms on the opposite side of the sheet (1.88 Å). In the stirrup conformer, the Si-C bond lengths are larger (1.92 Å) when H atoms are placed on the opposite side of the SiC plane. Also the Si-C distances in the stirrup conformation are higher compared to the Si-C distance in the chair form. This is to minimize the repulsion between the neighboring hydrogen atoms on the same side of the SiC sheet. However, the Si-H and C-H bond lengths are almost same for all the conformers. Importantly, all these conformers show symmetrical zigzag 2D structures, and a few of them resemble to those of graphane.8,98. J. O. Sofo, A. S. Chaudhari, and G. D. Barber, Phys. Rev. From the above discussion it is clear that the H atoms are bonded differently in various conformers of SiHCH and the relative stability of these isomers depends upon their bonding nature. The binding energy (BE) of the stable SiHCH conformer is calculated by the following equation:
BE=Etotal−n×ESi−n×EC−2n×EH4n(2)
where n is the number of Si/C atoms used for the optimization. The results suggest that the lowest energy structure of SiHCH sheet adopts chair conformation with binding energy −4.36 eV/atom. On the basis of the total energy criteria, the trend in stability of different conformers follows: chair > boat > puckered > twist > stirrup. Thus it may be inferred that the stability of different SiHCH conformers depends on the various arrangements of hydrogen atoms on the SiC honeycomb sheet. For graphane stirrup has an intermediate stability between chair and boat conformers. The bnding energies of chair, boat and stirrup conformers of graphene are 5.19, 5.14 & 5.17 eV, respectively, using PBE(GGA) exchange correlation.99. A. Bhattacharya, S. Bhattacharya, C. Majumder, and G. P. Das, Physical Review B 83, 033404 (2011). https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevB.83.033404 In this context it should be mentioned that for fully hydrogenated Si planar sheet also, the chair conformer is the most stable one.2626. Y. Ding and Y. Wang, App. Phys. Lett. 100, 083102 (2012). https://doi.org/10.1063/1.3688035 One of the reasons for the higher stability of the chair conformation is the minimum repulsion between the neighboring hydrogen atoms on the same side of the plane.
In order to understand the electronic structure of these conformers we have analyzed the densities of states (DOS) and the plots are shown in Fig. 4. The energy gap of the clean SiC sheet is found to be 2.42 eV.2727. S. Banerjee, S. Nigam, C. G. S. Pillai, and C. Majumder, Int. J. of Hydrogen Energy 37, 3733 (2012). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijhydene.2011.05.078 After hydrogenation, the energy gap of the SiC sheet widened with a concomitant increase in the intrinsic stability. The most stable chair conformer showed the energy gap of 3.7 eV. Among all the conformers, the puckered conformer showed the highest energy gap, which is calculated to be 4.2 eV. In order to estimate the point charge distribution, we have employed the Mulliken population analysis scheme using GAMESS software. It is found that, for all conformers, the Si donates electronic charges to C, and thereby creates polarization along the surface plane. This is unlike a graphane sheet, which forms non-polar surface. However, the charge distribution on the Si, C and H atoms depends on the geometrical arrangement of atoms in the specific conformer. In the chair conformer, while Si atom loses 0.4 electrons, carbons atoms gain 0.5 electronic charge from its nearest neighbors. The list of charge distribution on each atoms is summarized in Table II. The corresponding Mulliken charges for H atoms are 0.07e (attached with the Si atom) and 0.11e (attached with the C atom) respectively. It is to be noted that the charge transfer from C to H atoms is larger in comparison to the charge transfer between Si and H atoms.

The mean square deviation of C-H bond is found to be the least, which suggests that it is the strongest bond in the graphane like SiC sheet. In summary, we report the relative stability of various conformers of fully hydrogenated Si-C nano sheet. The calculation was carried out using plane wave based pseudopotential approach. Total eight different initial conformers were optimized. On the basis of total energy five conformers were found to be stable and specifically the chair conformer shows the highest stability. To verify the stability of the chair conformer we have performed MD simulation. The result suggests that the chair conformation remains stable even at room temperature.
