Abstract

Under partial shading conditions (PSCs), multiple local maximum power points (MPPs) may be exhibited on the P-U curve of photovoltaic systems. Direct control (DIRC) methods cannot extract the global MPP (GMPP); soft computing techniques can achieve it but are time consuming. This paper proposes a novel hybrid maximum power point tracking (MPPT) algorithm (INC-FA) combining incremental conductance (INC) and firefly algorithm (FA) to achieve better adaptability in various environments. INC is widely used because of its low-cost implementation and stability under rapidly changing atmospheric conditions, while FA is efficient in searching the GMPP. This combination (INC-FA) not only enables a faster global searching capability but also performs well as a DIRC method in the case of a single peak. In addition, INC-FA introduces the concept of the global optimal region and devises the population initialization mechanism to determine the initial position and population size of fireflies. Finally, the proposed algorithm is compared with three other MPPT methods under four different conditions. Simulation and experiment results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm can track the GMPP under various conditions with higher speed and accuracy.

Hybrid algorithms cannot automatically adjust their population sizes according to different environmental conditions, which would lead to low adaptability. Inadequate population size may be the main reason for the low tracking speed.
This paper proposes a novel hybrid algorithm (INC-FA) combining INC and FA to improve adaptability and tracking speed. Most of the existing algorithms have assumed that the population size should remain constant throughout the search process, but in the proposed algorithm, a new mechanism is adopted to adjust the population size to adapt to the current environment. First, the proposed algorithm calls INC to find the first LMPP rapidly. After that, the initial position and population size of fireflies would be decided by the population initialization mechanism (PIM). After the initialization, FA would be called to search the global optimal region (GOR). In the last part, the GMPP would be found by improved INC within GOR.

Finally, the summary of this paper is presented in Section VI.

III. BASICS OF P&O, INC, AND FA

In this work, we compare the performance of the proposed algorithm with P&O, INC, and FA. To facilitate discussion on this comparison, brief overviews of both methods are presented.
A. P&O
P&O is the most widely used MPPT method owing to its convenience. It is regarded as the standard benchmark for comparison. P&O is based on the following mechanism: The MPPT module controls the operating voltage of the PV array, and the operating voltage is perturbed in a given direction. If the power increases, then it means that the operating point has moved toward the MPP, and the operating point must be moved in the same direction of the next operation period; otherwise, it must be moved in the opposite direction.
A limitation of P&O is that the operating point oscillates around MPP, resulting in wastage of some amount of available energy.
B. INC
INC is classified as a DIRC method. This algorithm is derived by differentiating the output power of the PV array with respect to voltage. The main advantage of INC is that it provides a stable method under rapidly changing atmospheric conditions.2626. A. Loukriz, M. Haddadi, and S. Messalti, “ Simulation and experimental design of a new advanced variable step size incremental conductance MPPT algorithm for PV systems,” ISA Trans. 62, 30–38 (2016). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.isatra.2015.08.006 Also, it achieves lower oscillation than P&O.
The output power of PVs is given below:
P = U I . (6)
At the MPP, differentiating the PV power with respect to voltage and setting the result to zero gives
d P d U = I + U d I d U = 0. (7)
Incremental conductance is calculated as
d I d U = − I U , (8)
where
d I ≈ Δ I = I t 2 − I t 1 , (9)
d U ≈ Δ U = U t 2 − U t 1 . (10)
The subscripts t1 and t2 represent the physical quantities at two different moments. Hence, the position of the operating point can be judged in accordance with the following mechanism (see Figure 4):

  1. ΔI/ΔU = −I/U, the operating point is located at MPP.

  2. ΔI/ΔU > −I/U, the operating point is located at the left of MPP.

  3. ΔI/ΔU < −I/U, the operating point is located at the right of MPP.

The step size for the INC is generally fixed, and the power is drawn from the PV with a larger step size, contributing to faster dynamics; however, excessive steady state oscillations result in low search efficiency.2727. Q. Mei, M. W. Shan, L. Y. Liu, and J. M. Guerrero, “ A novel improved variable step-size incremental-resistance MPPT method for PV systems,” IEEE Trans. Ind. Electron. 58, 2427–2434 (2011). https://doi.org/10.1109/TIE.2010.2064275 Under this circumstance, on account of considering both the rapidity and effectiveness of INC, we have introduced a variable step size mechanism. It can improve the celerity and accuracy of speed tracking. In the region far away from the MPP, a large step size can be used to increase tracking speed. When the operating point is located near the MPP, a small step can improve tracking accuracy. Apparently, the step size of INC can be designed as a function of dP/dU, and the variable step size lk is given by the following equation:
l k = s d P k d U k , (11)
where s is the step size coefficient.
C. FA
FA is a novel metaheuristic algorithm proposed by Yang in Ref. 2828. X. S. Yang, Nature-Inspired Metaheuristic Algorithm ( Luniver Press, 2010).. FA is similar to PSO which is based on the swarm behavior of particles. PSO is widely used in MPPT for the PV system under PSCs because of its global search capability and ease of experimental implementation.2929. A. Khare and S. Rangnekar, “ A review of particle swarm optimization and its applications in solar photovoltaic system,” Appl. Soft Comput. 13(5), 2997–3006 (2013). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.asoc.2012.11.033 The work in Ref. 3030. X. S. Yang, “Firefly algorithms for multimodal optimization,” in Stochastic Algorithms: Foundations and Applications ( Springer, Berlin, Heidelberg, 2009), pp. 169–178. compares PSO with FA by employing several test functions. The analysis of the comparison indicates that FA is much more efficient in finding the global optima. Hence, compared with PSO, FA tends to be a better method for tracking GMPP.
FA is inspired by the flashing behavior of fireflies. The primary purpose for a firefly's flash is to act as a signal system to attract other fireflies. The brightness of a firefly is determined by the landscape of the objective function (power). The attraction between two fireflies is proportional to their brightness.
If the distance between the ith and jth fireflies is given by
r i j = ‖ x i − x j ‖ . (12)
The attraction degree β can be calculated by
β = β 0   exp   ( − γ r i j 2 ) , (13)
where β0 is initial attraction; γ is decrease in light intensity.
The position of the ith firefly on the kth iteration can be adjusted by
x i k = x i k − 1 + β ( x gbe − x i k − 1 ) + α ( r a n d − 0.5 ) , (14)
where xgbe is global best experienced position; α is random movement factor; rand is random number in the range [0, 1].

IV. PRINCIPLE OF INC-FA

INC is adapted for repaid search for a single peak, while FA has more powerful global search ability. As far as the proposed algorithm is concerned, INC and FA are combined for excellent results. The main advantage of the proposed algorithm is that it enables a quick switch between different search modes for single/multiple peaks. Hence, INC-FA can track change in external conditions rapidly. Additionally, it achieves a lower oscillation making the output power more stable. The proposed algorithm can be divided into three stages: pre-search, global search, and local search. Each stage is introduced as follows.
A. Pre-search stage
The population size and initial position of fireflies would be determined during the pre-search stage. A large population size of fireflies is advantageous for global search, but it would cause an excessive number of iterations and a slow search speed. On the contrary, a small population size could improve the search speed but the algorithm may fall at LMPP. Hence, it is important to adapt the population size to the current environment. We introduce a new mechanism (PIM) to optimize the initial location and the population size of fireflies.
1. Population initialization mechanism (PIM)
We can obtain the open circuit voltage of a PV array Uocs, and then, the approximate position of the first peak Um1 could be identified through the large step INC. xi means the position of ith firefly (see Figure 5).
x 1 = U m 1 . (15)
The population size of firefly n could be calculated by ([] represents rounding down)
n = [ U ocs − U m 1 Δ U p ] . (16)
Based on the position of first firefly x1, the initial positions of the others are given below:
x i = x 1 + ( i − 1 ) Δ U p , (17)
where ΔUp is the average distance among peak positions, and N is the number of PV modules in series:
Δ U p = 1 N U ocs . (18)
2. Method for judging uniform irradiation condition (UIC)
If the PV array is under UIC, the voltage at MPP (Um) should be satisfied with Equation (19). The PV voltage factor, MV, is an important characteristic of PV modules that could be used to determine if the array is under the UIC.
U m ≈ M V U oc . (19)
The voltage factor is usually assumed to be constant. However, with the change of temperature and irradiation, MV would fluctuate.3131. M. A. S. Masoum, H. Dehbonei et al., “ Theoretical and experimental analyses of PV systems with voltage and current-based maximum power point tracking,” IEEE Trans. Energy Convers. 17(4), 514–522 (2002). https://doi.org/10.1109/TEC.2002.805205 Hence, we can set a reasonable range as shown in Figure 6. If Um satisfies Equation (20), the PV array can be considered under UIC.
M V 1 U oc ≤ U m 1 ≤ M V 2 U oc , (20)
where MV1 and MV2 are the upper and lower limits of the voltage factor, respectively. (MV1 and MV2 could be set as MV1 = 0.7, MV2 = 0.8).

B. Global search stage
After the pre-search stage, FA would be called for global search. The search would stop when the global optimal region (GOR) is found (see Figure 7). GOR is defined as a contiguous area that could be established by a couple of fireflies at the maximum distance among all of them. The maximum distance dmax must be satisfied with Equation (21)
d max ≤ 2 5 Δ U p . (21)
After several iterations, all fireflies enter the GOR and the global best firefly xgbe is nearly equal to the GMPP. In this area, adaptive values of other fireflies are less than the global best. However, the iterations for massive fireflies in this area would take a long time.
C. Local search stage
In this situation, the improved INC would be started from the global best point xgbe to find GMPP. Assuming that the population size of fireflies is 4, we can still obtain a satisfactory result by the FA after 4 iterations (from n to n + 3) for all fireflies. It means that the PV system needs to change the operating point 16 times, as illustrated in Figure 8(a). In the same situation, improved INC only needs to change the operating point 4 times to reach the GMPP, as shown in Figure 8(b). The larger the firefly population size, the more time saved.

VII. CONCLUSION

This paper proposes a hybrid algorithm (INC-FA) to improve adaptability and tracking speed under different conditions. In order to avoid redundant fireflies and improve adaptability, a new mechanism (PIM) is adopted to solve this problem. PIM can automatically determine the initial position and the population size of fireflies according to the environment. Additionally, INC-FA adopts the strategy of searching GMPP through improved INC within GOR. This strategy can eliminate cumbersome iterations and improve the search speed. Finally, simulation and experimental results show that INC-FA can track GMPP quickly under various conditions, including UIC, PSC, and ESCC. Compared with the other three conventional algorithms, INC-FA has better adaptability and faster tracking speed.
