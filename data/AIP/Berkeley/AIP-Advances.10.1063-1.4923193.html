Abstract

Graphene continues to attract growing attention with its exceptional physical and mechanical properties, and more than one layer graphene structure with an orientation mismatch is often involved in practice. Using molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, we report the wettability of water droplet on a misoriented graphene bilayer structure. The contact angle of water droplet will change with the interlayer orientation of bilayer graphene structure, and reaches a maximum of 97.97 ± 1.15° at orientation mismatch of 40°. Comprehensive understanding on density profile and hydrogen bonding of water molecules and water-carbon interactive energy is explored to reveal the molecular mechanism.

II. COMPUTATIONAL MODEL AND METHODS

In the computational model, the graphene-like sheet was constructed by arranging carbon atoms on a hexagonal pattern with lattice constant of 0.142nm. Two rigid pristine graphene layers with each size of 12 nm×12 nm and typical interlayer distance of 0.34 nm were employed. The upper graphene layer was fixed and the bottom layer was manually rotated to form turbostratic graphene bilayer structure with an orientation mismatch, θR between them. Both of them were fixed in simulations so as to avoid any deviation of the misoriented angle after initial equilibrium of the system and quantitatively characterize the effect of the designed misoriented angle. Six different θR were considered and the overlaid patterns of bilayer graphene structure are illustrated in Figure 1(a). The 12-6 Lennard-Jones potential Vr=4εσ12/r12−σ6/r6 was used to model the non-bonded interactions, where ε is the potential well depth, σ is the equilibrium distance and r is the distance between two atoms. The SPC/E model was employed to model water molecules.21,2221. T. Werder, J. H. Walther, R. Jaffe, T. Halicioglu, and P. The cut-off radius of 1.0 nm was chosen for all L-J interactions to calculate the short-range van der Waals forces, and the particle-particle particle-mesh (PPPM) with a root mean of 0.0001 was used to handle the long range Coulomb interactions between oxygen and hydrogen atoms.20,2320. III. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION

To valid the accuracy of the present MD simulations, the water droplet on the graphene bilayer structure without orientation mismatch (i.e. θR = 0°&60°) was firstly performed. The corresponding contact angle is 94.30° ± 1.25°, showing excellent agreement with previous MD findings on the basis of the same simulation parameters and the same number of water molecules.2121. T. Werder, J. H. Walther, R. Jaffe, T. Halicioglu, and P. Koumoutsakos, The Journal of Physical Chemistry B 107, 1345 (2003). https://doi.org/10.1021/jp0268112 When the orientation mismatch angle θR changes from 0° to 60°, Figure 2 plots variations of the resultant contact angle θC. The measured contact angle θC shows a nonlinear change with θR, and reaches the maximum 97.97° ± 1.15° at θR = 40°, and the minimum 93.18° ± 1.45° at θR = 20°. In essence, the wettability of water molecules on graphene layers origins from the interactive energy between them. Therefore, the interactive energy between the water droplet and graphene was further explored. In calculations, the interactive energy of all water molecules to one adjacent carbon atom is superimposed to the position of carbon atom till to sweep all carbon atoms in both graphene layers, and the water molecules within the distance of 1.0nm (equals the cutoff radius used in MD simulation) from carbon atom were included. Figure 4 shows the energy mapping of water molecules- graphene bilayer structure, where only the contact region near the water droplet is given. At θR = 0° & 60° with a perfect alignment between two layers, Figure 4(a) shows an approximately uniform interactive energy distribution, and a similar energy distribution is also observed in Figure 4(d), which corresponds to a similar contact angle between them (Figure 2). There is the highest averaged energy at θR = 40° (Figure 4(e)) among six cases, implying the weakest constrained interaction of graphene to water molecules, consistent well with above density profile and hydrogen bond and contact angle in Figure 2.

IV. CONCLUDING REMARKS

The effect of interlayer orientation mismatch on the contact angle of water molecules is studied by employing graphene bilayer structure through molecular dynamics (MD) simulations. In comparison with the commensurate graphene bilayer structure without any orientation mismatch, the maximum difference of the resultant contact angle 4.79° on turbostratic graphene bilayer structure is probed. The molecular mechanism underpinned by interaction between water molecules and turbostratic graphene bilayer structure is revealed through the study of the water density profiles, the hydrogen bond and water-carbon interactive energy distribution, and shows good agreement with MD findings. The contact angle due to an interlayer orientation mismatch is expected to become significant when monolayer graphene is laid down on other 2D materials such as boron nitride (BN)28,2928. G.
